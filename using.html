Using This Site

This site works like a history magazine with a map: pick an era that interests you, and follow it across the world.

The deeper aim is to practice simultaneity — a feeling for what is happening now, in every sense of now. It's one thing to feel this today. It's another to practice it looking backward: imagine standing in the New World in 1300, with no idea the Old World exists at all. That sense — that our world is the whole of the world — still lives in us. Moving across timelines is a way to loosen it, a little, on purpose.

I'm also tracking patterns: the way an idea recurs across cultures wearing different clothes. Feathers, say — we might wave off a tribal headdress as "primitive," but feathers run through nearly every tradition: angels' wings, quill pens, ceremonial hats, the bird as messenger between worlds. Same thread, different knots.

How to explore
